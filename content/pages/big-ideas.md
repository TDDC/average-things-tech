Big Ideas is where Average Things Tech explores the larger patterns behind technology.

Not tutorials.

Not news.

Not hype.

This section is for thoughtful, evidence-aware essays about how technology affects people, habits, culture, learning, work, business, and society.

## What belongs here

Big Ideas is for articles that look beyond one device, app, operating system, or trend.

The goal is to understand the deeper pattern behind the surface-level technology discussion.

A Big Ideas article may explore questions like:

- Why do people react strongly to new tools?
- How do habits form around technology?
- Why do some systems feel simple while others feel exhausting?
- How do design choices affect normal users?
- What can history teach us about today’s technology debates?

## The rule

Interesting idea, but evidence-aware.

A Big Ideas article should not be a random rant.

It should try to connect:

- observation
- history
- research or examples
- practical meaning for normal people

Simple tech. Real use. No drama.
